Formula Reference (click to expand / collapse)

Complete drivetrain sizing chain: Motor → Bevel Gearbox → Worm Gear → Slewing Ring. Based on the Palfinger / Z-Systems sizing sheets.

{# ── Step 1 ── #}

1Worm shaft torque — maximum

{% latexify f1 math_block=True %}
M_max max crane slewing torque [Nm]
i_worm worm gear ratio
η worm gear efficiency (0–1)

Peak torque the worm input shaft must transmit. Used in Steps 3 and 6.

{# ── Step 2 ── #}

2Worm shaft torque — nominal

{% latexify f2 math_block=True %}
M_nom nominal (continuous) crane torque [Nm]
Same denominator as Step 1.

Used for continuous-duty / thermal sizing of the gearbox (Step 3).

{# ── Step 3 ── #}

3Required gearbox torque

{% latexify f3 math_block=True %}
S_f = 1.34 safety factor (Z-Systems sizing sheet)
M_2,nom from Step 2

The bevel gearbox catalogue torque rating must be ≥ this value. Example: ZK4 (760 Nm) > 517 Nm required.

{# ── Step 4 ── #}

4Bevel gearbox ratio

{% latexify f4 math_block=True %}
n_motor motor nameplate speed [rpm]
n_gear_out bevel output shaft speed [rpm]

Reduces motor speed to the worm input shaft speed. Used in Steps 6 and 9.

{# ── Step 5 ── #}

5Slewing speed

{% latexify f5 math_block=True %}
n_gear_out bevel output = worm input speed [rpm]
i_worm worm gear ratio

Final crane rotation speed. Typical target: 0.25–0.35 rpm for offshore cranes.

{# ── Step 6 ── #}

6Motor torque required

{% latexify f6 math_block=True %}
M_2,max worm shaft max torque from Step 1
i_bevel bevel ratio from Step 4

Minimum torque the motor must develop at starting to move the crane under peak load.

{# ── Step 7 ── #}

7Motor starting torque available

{% latexify f7 math_block=True %}
M_n motor rated (nameplate) torque [Nm]
k_start = Ma/Mn locked-rotor / rated torque ratio

For IEC induction motors Ma/Mn is typically 2.0–3.5; high-torque designs up to 4+.

{# ── Step 8 ── #}

8Torque feasibility check

{% latexify f8 math_block=True %}
{% latexify f8c math_block=True %}

The 1.3× margin accounts for supply-voltage dip, temperature rise, bearing stiction, and manufacturing tolerances.

{# ── Step 9 ── #}

9Motor power required

{% latexify f9 math_block=True %}
{% latexify f9b math_block=True %}
M_motor,req from Step 6 [Nm]  ·  n_motor [rpm]

Always round up to the next standard IEC frame power rating.

{# end 9-step row #}
